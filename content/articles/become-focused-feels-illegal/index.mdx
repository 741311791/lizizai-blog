# The Lost Art of Focus

In a world of constant distraction, the ability to focus is a superpower. Most people can't focus for more than 3 minutes without checking their phone. This is your competitive advantage.

## The Science of Deep Focus

When you enter deep focus (flow state), your brain releases a cocktail of neurochemicals:

- **Norepinephrine** - Heightens focus
- **Dopamine** - Increases motivation
- **Endorphins** - Block pain
- **Anandamide** - Promotes lateral thinking
- **Serotonin** - Creates well-being

This combination makes you literally smarter, more creative, and more productive.

## The Focus Protocol

### Environment Design

1. **Dedicated workspace** - Same place, same time
2. **Noise control** - Earplugs or brown noise
3. **Phone in another room** - Not on silent. In another room
4. **Single monitor** - Remove visual clutter

### The 90-Minute Block

Research shows the brain operates in 90-minute ultradian rhythms:

1. **Minutes 0-10** - Warm up, resist the urge to quit
2. **Minutes 10-30** - Entering the zone
3. **Minutes 30-60** - Deep flow
4. **Minutes 60-90** - Peak performance

After 90 minutes, take a 20-minute break. Then repeat.

### Technology That Helps

- **Website blockers** - Freedom, Cold Turkey
- **Focus music** - Brain.fm, Endel
- **Timer** - Physical kitchen timer (avoids phone)

## The Compound Effect

If you can do 4 hours of deep work daily, you'll outperform someone doing 10 hours of shallow work. In a year, that's 1,460 hours of focused creation vs. fragmented busyness.

Focus isn't a talent. It's a skill. And like all skills, it gets stronger with practice.
