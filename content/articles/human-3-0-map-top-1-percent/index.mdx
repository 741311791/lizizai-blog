# The Three Versions of Humanity

We're living through the most significant transition in human history. Not everyone will make the leap.

## Human 1.0: The Survivor

For most of human history, the goal was simple: survive. Find food, avoid danger, reproduce. This operating system served us well for 200,000 years.

But the world changed faster than our biology.

## Human 2.0: The Worker

The industrial revolution created a new template: go to school, get a job, retire at 65. Trade time for money. Follow instructions.

This system worked for a century. It's now breaking down.

## Human 3.0: The Creator

The next evolution is already here. Human 3.0 is characterized by:

- **Self-directed learning** over formal education
- **Creating value** over trading time
- **Leveraging technology** over competing with it
- **Building audiences** over climbing ladders
- **Systems thinking** over linear planning

## The Map to the Top 1%

### Step 1: Unlearn
Most of what you learned in school is wrong for this era. Start by questioning your assumptions about work, success, and value creation.

### Step 2: Build a Skill Stack
Don't be a specialist. Be a combinatorial generalist:
- Learn to write clearly
- Understand basic technology
- Develop sales and persuasion
- Master a creative discipline

### Step 3: Create Leverage
Leverage is the multiplier. Code and media are the two forms of permissionless leverage.

- **Code** - Write software that works while you sleep
- **Media** - Create content that compounds over time

### Step 4: Compound
Small daily actions, compounded over years, produce extraordinary results. The top 1% aren't smarter. They're more consistent.

## The Choice

You can stay in Human 2.0 mode, following the old script. Or you can upgrade. The choice is yours. But the window is closing.
