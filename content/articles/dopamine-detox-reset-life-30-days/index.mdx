# Why Your Brain Feels Foggy

If you're reading this, chances are your life has become a blur. You wake up, check your phone, scroll through feeds, and before you know it, hours have passed. You're not lazy. You're overstimulated.

## The Dopamine Problem

Modern life hijacks your brain's reward system:

1. **Social media** - Infinite scroll, variable rewards
2. **Streaming** - Auto-play, binge culture
3. **Fast food** - Engineered for cravings
4. **Notifications** - Constant interruption

Each of these triggers a dopamine spike. Over time, your baseline shifts. Things that should feel rewarding don't anymore.

## The 30-Day Reset Protocol

### Week 1: Awareness (Days 1-7)

Track every time you reach for your phone or open a social media app. Don't judge. Just observe.

### Week 2: Reduction (Days 8-14)

Set hard limits:
- Social media: 30 minutes total per day
- Entertainment: 1 hour maximum
- News: Once in the morning, done

### Week 3: Replacement (Days 15-21)

Fill the void with high-value activities:
- Read books (real ones, with pages)
- Exercise (even just walking)
- Create something (write, draw, code)
- Have real conversations

### Week 4: Integration (Days 22-30)

Build sustainable habits. You're not going monk mode forever. You're recalibrating.

## What Happens After

After 30 days, you'll notice:
- Better focus and concentration
- More energy throughout the day
- Deeper appreciation for simple pleasures
- Improved sleep quality

The goal isn't to eliminate dopamine. It's to reclaim control over what triggers it.
